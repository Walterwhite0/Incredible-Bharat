Alappuzha Kerala

Alappuzha is often referred to as the "Venice of the East" due to its intricate network of backwaters, lagoons, and canals. The backwaters are a unique ecosystem and a major attraction for tourists. Houseboats and traditional Kettuvallams offer a tranquil way to explore the scenic waterways.

Theyyam A Ritual Art popular in Kerla

Theyyam is a traditional and vibrant form of ritualistic performance art that is native to the northern part of Kerala, India, particularly in the Malabar region. It is a unique and deeply spiritual art form that combines elements of dance, music, and drama and is performed in honor of various deities, spirits, and heroes.

Ghora Katora Bihar

Ghora Katora Lake is a beautiful place and a hidden gem which is located in Rajgir, Bihar. "Ghora Katora" means "Horse Bowl" because the shape of the lake is like a Horse and the lake is surrounded by beautiful hills from three sides. And it is believed that during the time of Mahabharata, there used to be a stable of Jarasandha here.

Chandrashila Uttarakhand

Chandrashila is a destination that offers a blend of adventure, natural beauty, and spiritual significance. It is a must-visit place for those seeking a Himalayan trekking experience with stunning views and a touch of spirituality..

Shillong

Shillong is the capital city of the northeastern Indian state of Meghalaya and is often referred to as the "Scotland of the East" due to its scenic beauty and pleasant climate. It is known for its lush green hills, cascading waterfalls, and vibrant cultural scene.

Tsomgo Lake, Gangtok

Tsomgo Lake is not only a place of natural beauty but also a destination that offers visitors a serene and spiritual experience. It is a must-visit location for those exploring the beauty of the Himalayan region and the culture of Sikkim.

khajuraho

Khajuraho is home to a group of temples, which were built between the 9th and 11th centuries by the Chandela dynasty. These temples are known for their exceptional architectural design and intricate sculptures.

yellow Taxi

The yellow Ambassador taxis of Kolkata were a symbol of the city for decades. Their distinctive look and color made them instantly recognizable. Despite their decline, Ambassador taxis still hold a special place in the hearts of many Kolkatans and visitors who have fond memories associated with these iconic vehicles.

Gau Mata

Cows hold a significant and revered place in Hindu culture and religion. They are considered sacred and are often referred to as "Gau Mata" or "Mother Cow." The reverence for cows in Hinduism is deeply rooted in religious, cultural, and historical traditions

Rann Of Katch

The Rann of Kutch, also known as the Great Rann of Kutch, is a vast salt marsh located in the Thar Desert of the Indian state of Gujarat, near the border with Pakistan. It is one of the largest salt deserts in the world and covers an area of approximately 7,505 square kilometers (2,897 square miles).

Amber Fort Elephant Rides

One of the most famous places for elephant riding in Jaipur has been at the Amber Fort, where tourists could take elephant rides up the steep hill to the fort entrance. The elephants have traditionally been used as a mode of transportation for tourists.
